# Email Etiquette: Bioinformatics & Computational Biology

In the German interdisciplinary landscape, Bioinformatics sits at the intersection of the **Life Sciences** and **Informatics**. This guide covers the formal requirements of German academia while addressing the technical specifics of computational research.

---

## 1. The Subject Line (*Präzision ist Alles*)
In Bioinformatics, professors often juggle lab management, teaching, and dry-lab research. Your subject line must include the context (Course, Lab, or Server) and the specific technical issue.

* **Format:** `[Module/Project Name] – [Specific Technical Issue/Question] – [Name]`
* **Example (Course):** `Bioinformatics II (Modul 567) – Bug in Sequence Alignment Script – Jane Doe`
* **Example (Research/Thesis):** `Master Thesis – RNA-Seq Pipeline Optimization – Max Mustermann`

## 2. The Salutation (*Titel und Hierarchie*)
Even in "tech-heavy" labs, do not drop the formal titles unless explicitly told to do so.

| Role | Correct Salutation |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Professor / Lab Head** | `Sehr geehrte Frau Prof. Dr. [LastName]` |
| **PostDoc / Senior Researcher** | `Sehr geehrter Herr Dr. [LastName]` |
| **PhD Student / TA** | `Hallo [FirstName/LastName]` (Usually acceptable in lab settings, but use `Sehr geehrte(r)` for the first contact) |

## 3. Communication Style: The "Dry-Lab" Approach
German communication is direct. In Bioinformatics, this means providing enough data for the recipient to understand the problem without having to ask follow-up questions.

* **The Problem Description:** If you are reporting a bug or a pipeline error, don't just say "it doesn't work." State the tool, the environment (e.g., Conda, Docker, HPC), and the error message.
* **The "Sie" Factor:** Always use the formal *Sie* in your initial correspondence.
* **Code Snippets:** Never paste 100 lines of code into the body of an email. Use a link to a private GitHub Gist, a GitLab snippet, or attach a minimal reproducible example.

## 4. Specific Scenarios

### A. Requesting HPC/Server Access
When emailing a System Administrator or a Professor for cluster access, be professional and provide your technical "credentials" immediately.
* **Include:** Your SSH public key (if requested), your Matrikelnummer, and the specific project/lab you are associated with.

### B. Troubleshooting Assistance
Before emailing a Professor or TA about a coding issue, ensure you have checked the documentation.
* **The Phrase:** *"Ich habe bereits die Dokumentation von [Tool Name] geprüft, konnte jedoch keine Lösung für das Problem [Error] finden."* (I have checked the documentation but couldn't find a solution...)
